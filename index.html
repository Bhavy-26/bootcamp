The Art of Indian Pottery

Pottery is one of the oldest forms of art in India, dating back to the Indus Valley Civilization. It involves shaping clay into beautiful forms, which are then fired to achieve strength and durability. India is known for its diverse pottery styles, such as Terracotta from Rajasthan, Blue Pottery from Jaipur, and the famous Black Pottery from Manipur.

Each region in India has its own unique pottery tradition, often inspired by mythology, folklore, and nature. Pottery not only serves as a medium for artistic expression but also plays a significant role in daily life, from household items to religious rituals.

The Craft of Lac Bangles

Lac Bangles are an integral part of Indian tradition, particularly in Rajasthan and Bihar. These colorful bangles are made from lac, a natural resin secreted by insects on trees. The lac is heated, molded, and decorated with beads, mirrors, and intricate designs.

The making of lac bangles involves **melting the lac**, mixing it with **natural dyes**, and shaping it around a wooden stick before adding embellishments. These bangles are widely worn by married women, especially during festivals and weddings.
